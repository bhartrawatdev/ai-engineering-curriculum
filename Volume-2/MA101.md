## Course Description

MA101 covers single-variable calculus — limits, derivatives, and integrals — framed consistently around engineering application rather than pure abstraction. This is the first link in a mathematical chain that terminates in gradient-based machine learning: every derivative rule taught here reappears, unnamed but assumed, when students compute gradients by hand in AI201 and implement backpropagation in AI301.

## Why This Course Is Needed
Optimization is the mathematical core of machine learning. Before students can understand gradient descent, loss functions, or backpropagation, they need fluent, intuitive command of derivatives and integrals — not just the ability to apply memorized rules, but an understanding of what a derivative *means* (rate of change, slope, sensitivity).

## Prerequisite Knowledge Required
Pre-university algebra and trigonometry (assumed entry-level competency, not formally re-taught).

## Where It Is Used Later
- **MA103 (Calculus II, Semester 2)** — multivariable extension.
- **MA203 (Optimization Theory for ML, Semester 4)** — direct application of derivative rules to loss minimization.
- **AI301 (Deep Learning Foundations, Semester 5)** — backpropagation is chain-rule differentiation at scale.

## Learning Objectives

1. Compute limits and understand continuity.
2. Differentiate polynomial, trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions, including via product, quotient, and chain rules.
3. Apply derivatives to optimization (finding maxima/minima) and rate-of-change problems.
4. Compute definite and indefinite integrals of standard function classes.
5. Connect the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to the relationship between derivatives and integrals.

## Weekly Topics / Units

| Week | Topic |
|---|---|
| 1 | Functions review, domain/range, function composition |
| 2 | Limits: intuitive and formal definition, limit laws |
| 3 | Continuity, types of discontinuities |
| 4 | The derivative: definition as a limit, derivative as slope/rate of change |
| 5 | Differentiation rules: power, product, quotient rules |
| 6 | Chain rule, implicit differentiation |
| 7 | Derivatives of trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions |
| 8 | Applications: related rates |
| 9 | Applications: optimization (max/min problems) |
| 10 | Curve sketching: increasing/decreasing, concavity, inflection points |
| 11 | Introduction to integration: antiderivatives, indefinite integrals |
| 12 | Definite integrals, area under a curve (Riemann sums, conceptual) |
| 13 | Fundamental Theorem of Calculus |
| 14 | Integration techniques: substitution |
| 15 | Review, buffer, and connections to future optimization/ML use |

## Laboratory / Problem-Solving Sessions
Weekly problem-solving tutorial (not a coding lab) — worked problem sets reinforcing lecture content, run as recitation sections.

## Assignments
5 problem sets aligned to unit groupings (Weeks 1–3, 4–6, 7–9, 10–12, 13–14).

## Mini Projects
- **Applied Optimization Report (Week 9–10):** Students select a real-world scenario (e.g., minimizing packaging material, maximizing enclosed area with fixed fencing) and produce a short written solution using derivative-based optimization — first exposure to connecting pure math to an engineering-style deliverable.
