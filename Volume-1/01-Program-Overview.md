---

## 2. Graduate Attributes (Program Learning Outcomes)

On completion, graduates will be able to:

1. **Computational Foundations** — Reason about algorithms, data structures, and computational complexity.
2. **Mathematical Foundations for AI** — Apply linear algebra, probability, statistics, and optimization to AI problems.
3. **Software Engineering Competence** — Design, build, test, and maintain production-grade software systems.
4. **Machine Learning & Deep Learning Mastery** — Implement ML/DL systems from first principles and via modern frameworks.
5. **Systems & Production AI Engineering** — Deploy, scale, and monitor AI systems in real-world environments (MLOps, cloud, distributed systems).
6. **Research Literacy & Scientific Reasoning** — Read, critique, and reproduce results from AI research literature.
7. **Ethics, Safety & Responsible AI** — Evaluate AI systems for safety, fairness, and societal impact.
8. **Professional & Communication Skills** — Communicate technical work effectively in writing, code, and presentation.
9. **Lifelong Learning & Adaptability** — Independently acquire new AI tools, frameworks, and techniques.
10. **Collaborative & Project Leadership** — Work effectively in engineering teams using industry workflows (Git, code review, agile).

**Numbering convention:** First digit = Semester (e.g., `AI301` = Semester 5, third course in the AI stream, "01" running index). Semesters 1–2 use `1xx`, 3–4 use `2xx`, 5–6 use `3xx`, 7–8 use `4xx`.
